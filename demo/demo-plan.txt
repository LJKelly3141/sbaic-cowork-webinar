================================================================================
DEMO PLAN — Beyond the Chat Box: AI at Your Desk
Target: ~25 min total inside a ~60 min webinar
================================================================================

PRE-DEMO SETUP (do before going live)
================================================================================

  - Open Claude Desktop in Cowork mode
  - Have a sandbox folder ready at demo/sample-business-folder/ with
    3-5 plausible files (invoice, email .txt, memo, spreadsheet)
  - Start a fresh Cowork session (no prior history)


DEMO 1: WHAT'S IN MY FOLDER?  (~12 min)
================================================================================

Folder: demo/sample-business-folder/

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE PROMPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Read everything in this folder. Then tell me:

1. What's here -- a short index of files with one-line descriptions
2. What needs my attention this week -- anything time-sensitive,
   decisions owed, or follow-ups overdue
3. What's not here that probably should be -- gaps you'd ask me about

Don't write any files yet -- just report back.

--- NARRATE AS COWORK WORKS ---

  - "Notice Cowork is opening each file itself -- I didn't have to upload them."
  - "It's planning a sequence of actions first. That's the agentic part."
  - When the punch list appears: "This came from reading actual files, not
    guessing from a description."

--- FOLLOW-UP (if time) ---

For the items you flagged as needing attention this week, draft a short
reply or response for each one. Save the drafts as a single Word document
in this folder called Drafts_For_Review.docx.


DEMO 2: SAVING WORK AS A SKILL  (~13 min)
================================================================================

Run in the same Cowork session.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPTION A -- SAVE THE DEMO 1 PROMPT AS A CUSTOM SKILL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Save what we just did as a skill called "Monday Briefing." It should:

  - Read the current working folder
  - Produce the same 3-part report
  - Run once per week -- set it up as a Monday morning scheduled task

Tell me what you've saved and how I trigger it next week.

Then open a fresh chat and type /monday-briefing to demonstrate.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPTION B -- USE A PRE-BUILT SKILL FROM THE MARKETPLACE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Show me what skills I have available right now. Pick one that's relevant
for a small business owner -- maybe inbox triage or a weekly briefing --
and walk me through using it on this folder.

--- NARRATE ---

  - "The saved skill turns 200 words of instruction into one command.
    That's the repetition tax going away."
  - "Cowork can also install skills from a marketplace -- Anthropic and
    other publishers ship skills you can use right away."
  - "Scheduled tasks mean Cowork runs while you're not at your desk."

--- FOLLOW-UP ---

What other workflows in my week look like good candidates to turn into
skills? Don't build them -- just suggest 3 or 4 based on what you've seen.


================================================================================
PRESENTER NOTES
================================================================================

- Show the folder picker on screen when starting -- the "Work in a folder"
  visual makes the whole concept click.
- Don't rush past the planning step. When Cowork shows its plan, read it
  aloud -- that's the "auditable" promise.
- If Cowork asks for approval to write a file, narrate that out loud:
  "That's the safety guardrail."
- If something fails live, use it: "That's actually a common scenario."
- If behind time: skip Demo 1 follow-up and the "what other workflows" Q.
  The save-as-skill moment is the lesson -- protect it.
